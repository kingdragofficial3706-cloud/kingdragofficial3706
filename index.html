Bioactive Compound Extraction Kinetics

Utilizing Calculus to Optimize Essential Oil Recovery from Malaysian Agro-Biomass

🌿 The Bioscience Scenario

In agro-product processing, maximizing the extraction efficiency of valuable bioactive ingredients (such as antioxidants, polyphenols, or essential oils) is vital. This simulation tracks the microwave-assisted recovery yield percentage ($Y$) of essential oils over extraction time ($t$, in minutes).

By pinpointing the exact rate of extraction using derivatives, processing engineers can prevent compound thermal degradation and reduce energy expenditures.

Empirical Framework: Modeled after standard solid-liquid mass transfer trends found in the Journal of Advanced Research in Fluid Mechanics and Thermal Sciences (Kinetic Studies of Essential Oil Extraction from Malaysian Biomass).

📊 Mathematical Modeling

Yield Function, $f(t)$
$f(t) = -0.008t^2 + 0.48t$

Estimates total accumulated essential oil yield percentage over time.

First Derivative, $f'(t)$
$f'(t) = -0.016t + 0.48$

Calculates the instantaneous extraction rate (% yield per minute).
